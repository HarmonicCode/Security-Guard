The Role of Security Guards

Besides acting as entrance door guardians, security guards offer a wide and diverse list of functions. Their responsibilities include:

1. Crime Prevention

The presence of security personnel to the extent that they can be seen by the public is always a big put-off to any criminal. Guards are trained both to observe the presumptive signs of security threats and to prevent threats from occurring.

2. Access Control

They patrol around compound areas to prevent people who have no business within areas of the compound from accessing the area of the compound. This is very relevant for any business, residential estates especially the compounds, and highly secured areas.

3. Emergency Response

During an emergency such as fire, medical emergencies, or security issues, security guards are expected to act appropriately. This means that their capability to act at the same place and at the same time to make split decisions can reduce the harm and stop escalation.

4. Surveillance and Monitoring

Security personnel are entrusted with CCTV security, moving around the premises and looking for areas that could be exploited. Their approach means that all risks are managed ahead of time to avoid being turned into threats.

5. Customer Interaction

Receptionists and security personnel are also in charge of the reception of guests. This way, the professionalism and friendliness of the staff members help make the process enjoyable while keeping it safe.

6. Conflict Resolution

In the case of employee conflicts, or customer or resident conflicts, security guards are experienced in handling conflicts and enforcing peace.
