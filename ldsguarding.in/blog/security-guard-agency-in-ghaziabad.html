Core Responsibilities of a Security Guard

While individual security guard duties vary depending on the location and purpose, several core responsibilities define their role:

Crime Deterrence: A security guard is a visible physical control over trespass and other Criminal Activities in the premises. This tamer’s presence minimizes opportunities for shoplifting, malicious damage to property, and other unlawful activities that make the environment insecure.

Surveillance and Monitoring: Guards are programmed to watch their environment and when there is anything amiss they follow it up by direct observation and/or with the help of technologies such as Closed Circuit Television Cameras (CCTV). This makes it easy to prevent any other probable security issues from happening.

Access Control: One of the most crucial tasks of security guards is to control access to/ from points by permitting only the right persons to access a facility. This includes identity checks, registration of visitors, and another check as may be required.

Emergency Response: Security guards are equally involved in emergencies such as medical adversity, fires, or natural disasters. They are taught how to react, help those who might need help, and how organize actions with the services, which should respond to the emergency.

Customer Service: Apart from security duties, the guards themselves ensure compliance with security measures, assist with navigation, explain anything a visitor might not understand, and ensure cordiality. This is especially the case in commercial and residential facilities where guards are also in charge of customer relations.

Patrol Services: This is because guards often move around the premises to inspect any suspicious activities as well as to confirm that every part is well guarded. These patrols can be planned, but they could also be done on a random basis to try and cover those areas that are most likely to be targeted by potential aggressors.
