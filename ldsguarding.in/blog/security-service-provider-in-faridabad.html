Key Functions Accomplished by a Security Guard

Being a security guard goes beyond just standing at the entrance and waving the occasional car. Some core functions include:

Crime Prevention: The first of these tasks that a security guard is assigned is to prevent criminal activity through observation presence. Equipped to identify any signs of the evil doers they can avoid such people and acts of criminality.

Surveillance and Monitoring: Guards are responsible for the observation of security systems, television cameras, and alarms to guarantee all the areas of the premises are under observation. It is important to identify deviations from normal conventions before they turn into security vices.

Access Control: Security guards monitor the gates and who comes in and goes out and only those who are allowed are allowed in. They check everyone, record visitors, and ensure that they conform to the laid down security procedures.

Emergency Response: Security guards are responsible for responding to incidents that may erupt in the facility such as fire, medical emergencies, or natural calamities. Able to give first aid and know what has to be done until professional emergency services arrive, and they are trained.

Customer Service: Conducting security checks, they also act as porters and information and directions providers for those who come to the institution. Among these customer service aspect is most important in providing businesses and residents with a safe environment.

Patrol and Inspection: Employees at all times guarding and moving around the premises to ensure there is order throughout the compound. These patrols can be periodic or random because when a criminal element is looking to take advantage of a weak spot they can never be sure of when the patrols will be passing by.

Reporting and Communication: Another important function that people mostly fail to remember is documentation. Security guards are supposed to compile records of incidences, visitors, and the checkpoints they conduct.
